Natalia Pacheco Tallaj

I'm a second-year PhD student at MIT under the supervision of Søren Galatius and Mike Hopkins. My interests lie somewhere between algebraic and geometric topology, and at times their applications to physics. Currently, I'm working on two projects related to K-theory and manifolds.

teaching & service

During my time at MIT, I have had three fantastic directed reading or UROP students, in category theory and in algebraic topology (specifically, K-theory), and three awesome GUMMI mentees. I serve in the GUMMI board, Women in Math committee, the Math Department Diversity Committee, and I co-founded Spec(MIT) with other grad students. Outside MIT, I regularly teach at the Puerto Rico Math Olympiad's training and outreach efforts, and I have served as delegation leader for the Puerto Rican team to the IberoAmerican Math Olympiad and the IMO.
